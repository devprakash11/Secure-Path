Frequently Asked Questions
How to Create an Online Classroom +

'Secure Path online classroom offers a secure & centralized way for you to assign quizzes & courses to your learners. For your learners, it means a single platform where they can access courses and view reports (including past scores), certificates, and more.

  • Step 1: On your Secure Path Training Maker dashboard, go to Classroom.
  • Step 2: Click + New Classroom.
  • Step 3: Give a name to your classroom and click Add. Your classroom is now ready.
  • Step 4: On the My Classroom screen, click Add Users to start adding learners to your classroom.
  • Step 5: Add users to the box as instructed and click Next.
How to Create an Online Quiz +

To add a quiz to your course, choose "Quiz" from the "Add New" menu and select the quiz you want to embed. In case you haven't created a quiz yet, click the "+Add Quiz" button to create one.

Chapters are designed to organize your course, not hold content directly. To upload files like PDFs or presentations, add a page within the chapter. Only pages support file uploads and media integration.

Can I issue different certificates for different learning paths? +

Not currently within one course, but you can split a path into separate short courses and assign unique certificates to each.

Is there a way to track who downloaded their certificate? +

Certificate downloads aren't tracked individually, but you can confirm course completion in the reports section.

Do certificates expire? +

Expiration isn't built into certificates, but you can display validity dates manually or trigger a follow-up course using automation when the certificate is about to expire.

How do I prevent people from guessing their way through? +

You can add retry limits, randomize questions, set timers, and even enable feedback suppression until all attempts are used.

What if learners are in low-connectivity areas? +

Some teams shorten quizzes and split modules, so users can attempt small chunks. For remote learners, this helps avoid starting over if their connection drops.
